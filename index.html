Pebble Stones

Pebble stones are small, rounded stones that are naturally formed by water, usually through the process of erosion. They come in a variety of shapes, colors, and sizes, and are commonly found in riverbeds, beaches, or lakeshores.

Because of their smooth texture and attractive appearance, pebble stones are often used for landscaping, decorative garden features, walkways, and even in aquariums or water features.

They can also be used indoors for things like mosaic tiles, backsplashes, and accent pieces. The stones are available in a range of colors, including gray, white, black, beige, and even multicolored varieties.

Pebble Stone Paving

Pebble stone paving is a decorative and functional technique that uses natural or artificial pebbles embedded in a concrete or resin base to create visually appealing surfaces. It's a popular choice for outdoor and indoor spaces due to its aesthetic charm, durability, and versatility.

Characteristics of Pebble Stone Paving:

  • Natural Appearance: Pebbles come in various shapes, sizes, and colors, allowing for unique and natural designs.
  • Material Variety: Typically uses river stones, quartz, marble, or basalt pebbles, available in polished or unpolished finishes.
  • Durability: Strong and long-lasting, especially when combined with resin or concrete.
  • Textured Surface: Provides a non-slip surface, making it ideal for areas exposed to water or foot traffic.
  • Customizable: Can be designed in patterns, mosaics, or uniform spreads.

Common Uses of Pebble Stone Paving:

  • Driveways: Durable and visually appealing, it can handle vehicular traffic.
  • Walkways and Pathways: Used in gardens, parks, and residential areas for aesthetic walkways.
  • Patios and Decks: Creates a relaxing, natural ambiance.
  • Pool Surrounds: The non-slip surface and water resistance make it ideal for pools.
  • Interior Floors and Walls: Adds a luxurious touch to bathrooms, spa areas, or accent walls.
  • Landscaping: Used for edging flower beds, water features, or decorative garden designs.

Natural Cut Stones

Natural cut stone refers to stone that has been quarried and shaped into specific dimensions or forms for use in construction, decoration, or landscaping. Unlike rough or unprocessed stone, cut stone is carefully worked to achieve a desired size, shape, and finish while retaining its natural characteristics.

Characteristics of Natural Cut Stone:

  • Variety of Materials: Common types include limestone, sandstone, granite, marble, travertine, and slate. Each offers unique properties like color, texture, and durability.
  • Precision: Cut stones are processed into precise sizes and shapes for easy installation and a polished appearance.
  • Surface Finishes: Options include polished, honed, bush-hammered, flamed, tumbled, or natural cleft, depending on the intended use.
  • Durability: Suitable for long-term use in various applications.
  • Color and Texture: Available in a wide range of colors, veining, and textures based on the stone type and quarry source.
